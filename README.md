## Academic Honesty
The course's approach to academic honesty is best described as "be reasonable." You are encouraged to discuss material with classmates and seek help when needed as this is a normal part of learning. However, there is a clear line between getting help and submitting someone else's work as your own.

All work you submit must fundamentally be your own. You may ask others for guidance, but that guidance should never result in someone else completing your work for you. When seeking help, you may show your work to others, but you may not view theirs. Collaboration on the final project is permitted only to the extent described in its specification.

If you are ever unsure whether something is allowed, ask the instructor **before** doing it, not after. Violations are taken seriously and may result in a grade of 0 for the work in question, or a failing grade for the course entirely.

### Reasonable
- Discussing course material with classmates to better understand it.
- Asking a classmate for help identifying an issue in your work, provided you cite their help.
- Seeking help from a tutor, provided the tutor does not do your work for you.
- Using online resources for learning and reference, but not for direct solutions to assignments.
- Using AI tools to support your understanding, not to complete your work for you.
- Reviewing past exams or quizzes made available by the instructor.
- Resubmitting your own prior work, provided you disclose this clearly in your submission.

### Not Reasonable
- Viewing another student's solution before submitting your own.
- Submitting work that is not your own, with or without modifications.
- Sharing your solution with a classmate who has not yet submitted.
- Searching for or using outright solutions to assignments from the internet or any other source.
- Splitting an assignment with another student and combining your work.
- Using AI tools to generate answers that you submit as your own.
- Submitting the same work to another course without prior approval from the instructor.
- Failing to cite sources, tools, or assistance used in your work.
